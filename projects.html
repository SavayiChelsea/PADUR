Remote School Visits

Reaching the most vulnerable children in isolated communities

25+
Schools Visited
2,500+
Children Reached
15
Remote Areas

Dr. Debbie Maloba, accompanied by her coordinator and committee members, conducts regular visits to challenged schools in the most remote areas of the DRC. These visits are crucial for assessing educational needs, providing resources, and establishing long-term support systems for children who would otherwise lack access to quality education.

Our team evaluates infrastructure needs, teacher training requirements, and student welfare conditions. Each visit results in a comprehensive action plan tailored to the specific challenges faced by each school community.

Key Achievements:

  • Established sustainable supply chains for educational materials
  • Implemented teacher mentorship programs
  • Provided emergency nutritional support
  • Built partnerships with local communities
  • Created scholarship programs for exceptional students

Soap Making Training Program

Empowering vulnerable populations through skill development in conflict zones

150+
People Trained
45
Refugees Supported
30
People with Special Needs

In the challenging environment of Goma, DRC - a region affected by ongoing conflict - PADUR provides soap making training to some of the most vulnerable members of society. Our program specifically targets people with special needs and refugees, offering them practical skills that can generate immediate income.

This initiative not only provides economic opportunities but also contributes to community health by increasing the availability of hygiene products in areas where they are often scarce and expensive.

Program Benefits:

  • Sustainable income generation for vulnerable populations
  • Improved community hygiene standards
  • Skills that can be transferred to family members
  • Reduced dependency on external aid
  • Creation of local micro-businesses

Annual Meetings

Led by Aurelie Biselenge, PADUR's dedicated coordinator, our annual meetings serve as vital platforms for community engagement, transparency, and collaborative planning. These gatherings bring together community leaders, beneficiaries, and stakeholders to review progress and plan future initiatives.

These meetings ensure that our programs remain community-driven and responsive to local needs. They also provide opportunities for skill-sharing between different communities and the celebration of collective achievements.

Meeting Outcomes:

  • Community-driven project prioritization
  • Transparent reporting on program outcomes
  • Inter-community knowledge sharing
  • Stakeholder feedback integration
  • Annual strategic planning

Formation en Entrepreneuriat

Empowering women through business training and microfinance

17
Women Trained
$85,000
Total Investment
15
Active Businesses

Our comprehensive entrepreneurship training program has successfully graduated 17 women, each receiving $5,000 in startup capital to launch their own businesses. This transformative initiative combines business education with practical financial support to create sustainable economic opportunities.

The program covers business planning, financial management, marketing strategies, and operational excellence. Participants receive ongoing mentorship and support as they establish and grow their enterprises, creating a ripple effect of economic empowerment in their communities.

Success Metrics:

  • 88% business survival rate after 12 months
  • Average monthly income increase of 400%
  • 50+ indirect jobs created
  • Community economic multiplier effect
  • Mentorship network established
